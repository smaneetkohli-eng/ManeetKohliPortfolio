Data Tracking

For this data tracking activity, I focused on measuring my daily sleep score over the course of several months, setting a goal of 85 points per night. I collected this data using my Garmin Forerunner 265, logging each night’s result and compiling it into charts to better visualize patterns and trends over time.

After collecting the data, I analyzed both daily consistency and overall averages. The visualizations made it easy to identify which nights I hit my goal and where I fell short, reinforcing how small, consistent habits accumulate into meaningful long-term patterns.

To better understand these results, I used line and bar charts to highlight trends. Seeing the data laid out visually made patterns clear in a way raw numbers alone never could.

Team Presentation

This team presentation, created for my Business Communication course, focuses on how to effectively build and expand your professional network using a well-crafted network pitch. In the video, we break down what a network pitch is, who your network is, why it is essential in professional settings, and how it can be applied in multiple scenarios. We walk through the key components of a strong pitch — introducing yourself clearly, communicating your goals, and creating a memorable impression — while also emphasizing the importance of authenticity and confidence.

Email Activity

In this email project, we were asked to revise, rewrite, and share an improper email so that it follows the email etiquette you would expect to find in most organizations. My primary goal in my revisions were to change the negative tone, organize the message so that it can be read seamlessly, and improve the overall clarity by eliminating unnecessary comments — all while keeping the original message intact.

This activity gave me the ability to improve on my business communication by adapting my writing to a more professional liking.
